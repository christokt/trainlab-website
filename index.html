Welcome to the TRAIN Lab @ WPI

The Trustworthy and Resilient Artificial Intelligence and Networks (TRAIN) Lab at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) is a research group dedicated to building trustworthy, resilient, and intelligent systems for next-generation networking and computing systems. We explore foundational and applied challenges at the intersection of artificial intelligence, wireless communication, and cyber-physical systems, with a focus on enabling generalizable, explainable, and sustainable AI for 6G and beyond.

Our work blends theory, systems, and interdisciplinary insights, from explainable AI, neuroscience-inspired modeling, game theory, and Bayesian inference to real-world network deployment challenges. We are always looking for passionate PhD students and collaborators who are excited about pushing the boundaries of AI-native networked systems.

Our Research Areas

Semantic Communication

Communication systems that convey meaning and task-relevant information rather than only raw bits.

Foundation Models for Networks

AI-native network intelligence using large-scale models for reasoning, adaptation, and control.

Mathematical Foundations of AI

Principled tools for trustworthy, generalizable, and interpretable AI systems.

Integrated Sensing and Communication

Joint sensing, communication, and computation for next-generation wireless and cyber-physical systems.
